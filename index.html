Overview of UK Financial Regulation

The UK maintains a comprehensive regulatory framework for financial services:

  • FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) - Conducts regulation
  • PRA (Prudential Regulation Authority) - Prudential oversight
  • Bank of England - Monetary policy and financial stability
  • Treasury - Government financial policy

Key Regulatory Frameworks

  • GDPR - Data protection and privacy requirements
  • PSD2 - Payment Services Directive 2 (Open Banking)
  • MiFID II - Markets in Financial Instruments Directive
  • RMKTD - Reporting Markets Technical Directive
  • FCA Handbook - Comprehensive rulebook

GDPR Compliance in Finance

Personal data protection is paramount:

  • Consent Requirements - Explicit user consent for data processing
  • Data Subject Rights - Access, rectification, erasure rights
  • Privacy by Design - Integrate data protection from the start
  • DPA 2018 - UK's implementation of GDPR
  • Data Processing Agreements - Contracts with third parties

Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)

Required under PSD2 for electronic payments and sensitive account access:

  • Two-Factor Authentication - SMS, App, Card, Biometric
  • Exemptions - Low value payments, recurring transactions (regulated)
  • Implementation Deadline - 31 December 2020 (extended for certain scenarios)
  • Risk-Based Approaches - Reduced friction for low-risk transactions

Financial Data Integration Best Practices

  • API-First Architecture - Use secure, standardized APIs (OAuth 2.0)
  • Encryption - TLS 1.2+ for transit, AES-256 for storage
  • Access Controls - Role-based, least privilege principles
  • Audit Logging - Complete record of all data access
  • Regular Testing - Penetration testing and security audits

Compliance Monitoring & Reporting

Continuous oversight requirements:

  • Transaction Monitoring - AML/CFT compliance
  • Regulatory Reporting - EMIR, REMIT, TRACE equivalents
  • Incident Reporting - Security breaches within 72 hours to ICO
  • Regular Audits - Internal and external assessments

Key Takeaways

  • UK regulation prioritizes consumer protection and market stability
  • PSD2 and Open Banking drive innovation in financial services
  • GDPR compliance is non-negotiable for data handling
  • Strong authentication and encryption are fundamental
  • Continuous monitoring and reporting demonstrate compliance
